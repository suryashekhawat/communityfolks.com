Live Community Blogging Meetup

Yet again, our monthly “community meetup” brought us some amazing questions and discussions. Our theme for this month was putting “Live Community Blogging”. Never have we ever put up any meetup discussion online and we realised how much we are missing on! Interestingly, this meetup started off by individual introductions and a bunch of questions that were not reckoned within our 2 hour meetup!


Let’s dig in:
How can we cross collaborate for communities? The question was raised by Malavikka who handles a community of over 100+ married women. In her words, “ I have handled the community for over a year now, but to get everyone involved, I run contests every now and then. Recently, I thought to make each member plant a tree as part of a contest. Now, I think the contribution would have been much more if I could collaborate with another community or brand. How to achieve that?” We gathered a lot of insights from Rochelle, Ankita, and Paras.

According to Paras, it is best to find a common platform where you can post and collaborate with other brands. Create an open event on their page, publish it on their wall, and make them aware about your event.

Second, another suggestion that came in by Ankita was to target smaller or local brands. It seems so obvious that if you want to collaborate with bigger brands, they tend to look on many aspects like your social media engagement, your followers, how big your brand is, or how much ROI are you capable of bringing them. Instead, why not just target local brands initially. They will build trust with you and it brings a larger audience for both of you.

Third, even if you want your main collaborative brand to be one of the “bigger” ones then make sure your marketing is done right and here content plays the role. Your pitch is just right and you offer them crazy deliverables. Along with that, plan ahead and gather insights which gives them a good 6-month plan in hand. Everyone likes the packaging to be minimal but perfect. Make sure that happens.

2. How to attract people without a brand name?
A lot of people suggested to incentivize the meetup or the occasion where you wish to hero the brand. Example, in a sports event make the Red Bull hero by advertising it on social media with your players, posting pictures with it, and making content the hero. Recognize your people in the group and give them due credit for their involvement. The exact aspect Ankita uses in Progate community as well.

3. How to attract students to pay for the courses that you have to offer?
As Meet suggested, the best part about getting students involved for your campaigns is to incentivize them and that doesn’t involve monetary incentives. It’s about providing them with industrial experience, a certificate of training, or appointing them as an intern. In this way, they scale their resumes and build a trust relationship with you and your community and probably then paying for a course doesn’t sound like a bad idea. Ankita, came up with an interesting analogy. If it is pie from 0-100, we as a community build the students from 0-50 and pitch them to companies. The star performers get a chance in big industries and they train them to become 90 from 50. An amazing approach of collaboration with mentoring. Such kind of skillful mentoring always helps the students to grow wisely.


Oh! So many questions and so many approaches which is hard to confine into a small box. I have managed to capture the gist of it and I hope whoever is reading this gets the best out of it.

Keep attending such meetups because there is just so much to take away and learn and apply.

Get ready for the next meetup soon. Adios.

Thank you, Paras, Meet, Dhruv, Rochelle, Shraddha, Malavikka, Azhar, Pradyumana, Ankita, Grishma, Hamlet for gracing the event with integrity.
